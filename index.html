Honest About the Technology

Not AI the way
you're picturing it.

The counsel is built on a special kind of AI — one that searches and synthesizes sound biblical truths from across two thousand years of church history, from Augustine to Jay Adams. It doesn't speak from nowhere or make things up. Using a method called Retrieval-Augmented Generation, your guide draws on a curated library of Scripture and trusted Christian writings, then answers from those sources. When you ask about anxiety, marriage, depression, or addiction, you receive guidance that is consistent, grounded, and faithful — the historic voice of the church, speaking to your situation.

The Counselor Resource Library

Two thousand years
of Christian counsel.

Your guide draws from Scripture and a curated library of historic Christian pastoral and theological writings — voices from the early church, the Reformation, the Puritans, and the great evangelical tradition that followed.

Scripture

  • The Holy Bible — the full New Testament, plus the Psalms, Proverbs, Isaiah, Job, Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon, and Genesis 1–11

The Early Church

  • Augustine of Hippo — Confessions; On Christian Doctrine

The Reformation

  • Martin Luther — letters and devotional writings
  • John Calvin — Institutes of the Christian Religion; Commentary on the Psalms; The Golden Booklet of the Christian Life

Confessions & Catechisms

  • The 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith
  • The Westminster Confession and Catechisms (Larger and Shorter)
  • The Three Forms of Unity
  • The Heidelberg Catechism
  • Keach's Catechism (1677)

The Puritans

  • John Bunyan — The Pilgrim's Progress; Grace Abounding
  • John Flavel — Keeping the Heart
  • John Owen — The Mortification of Sin
  • Richard Sibbes — The Bruised Reed
  • Thomas Watson — All Things for Good

The Evangelical & Pastoral Tradition

  • Jonathan Edwards — Religious Affections
  • Andrew Murray — With Christ in the School of Prayer; Absolute Surrender; The True Vine
  • J. C. Ryle — Holiness; Expository Thoughts on the Gospels (Matthew)
  • Charles Spurgeon — Morning and Evening; The Treasury of David; Faith's Checkbook; All of Grace; Till He Come

Modern Pastoral

  • John Piper — Filling Up the Afflictions of Christ
  • Joni Eareckson Tada — selected writings on suffering and the sovereignty of God
  • Paul David Tripp — selected articles and devotionals
  • Michael Reeves — selected addresses on identity in Christ and the image of God

Modern Biblical Counseling

  • Jay Adams — selected articles from the Institute for Nouthetic Studies
  • John Babler — biblical crisis and trauma counseling (ACBC Truth in Love)
  • Rosaria Butterfield — selected articles on sin, identity, and repentance
  • Association of Certified Biblical Counselors — Truth in Love (selected episodes)

When you bring a question, your guide draws from this library — the historic voice of the church speaking to your situation — and weaves it together with Scripture to point you to Christ, the Great Physician.

The Kiln Ministries

Formed in the fire.
For the glory of God.

The Kiln Ministries is a nonprofit ministry based in Jackson, Tennessee, committed to equipping believers with tools for biblical growth, counseling, and discipleship. Great Physician is one expression of that mission — bringing the healing Word of God to souls in need, wherever they are.

A Note of Honesty

Great Physician is not a replacement for your local church or pastor.

Your guide is a tool to help you begin the journey toward biblical change. We will always encourage you to be part of a local congregation and to sit under faithful pastoral care. AI will never replace a physical local church and in-person counseling and discipleship.
