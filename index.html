Meet Your Counselors

Choose the voice that
will guide you through God's Word.

All four companions are grounded in Scripture and trained in the nouthetic counseling tradition. Each will speak the truth in love — and each will always point you to Jesus Christ, the Great Physician.

Barnabas

Acts 4:36

A son of encouragement. Barnabas walks alongside you with truth and grace from God's Word — warm, honest, and always pointing to Christ.
Ethan

1 Kings 4:31 · Psalm 89

A man of deep wisdom. Ethan anchors you in God's covenant faithfulness — steadfast, theologically rich, and rooted in the enduring promises of Scripture.
Lydia

Acts 16:14

A woman of prayer and faith. Lydia listens with a caring heart, makes you feel deeply known, and gently leads you to the healing found in Jesus.
Anna

Luke 2:36–38

A godly woman devoted to prayer and fasting. Anna helps you see Christ more clearly — turning your eyes from the circumstances of life to the Savior who redeems them.

Our Library and Sources

Two thousand years
of Christian counsel.

Every counselor draws from Scripture and a curated library of historic Christian pastoral and theological writings — voices from the early church, the Reformation, the Puritans, and the great evangelical tradition that followed.

Scripture

  • The Holy Bible — the full New Testament, plus the Psalms, Proverbs, Isaiah, Job, Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon, and Genesis 1–11

The Early Church

  • Augustine of Hippo — Confessions; On Christian Doctrine

The Reformation

  • Letters and devotional writings of the Reformation (Luther's correspondence, Calvin's Golden Booklet and Psalms commentary)

Confessions & Catechisms

  • The 1689 Baptist Confession of Faith
  • The Westminster Confession and Catechisms (Larger and Shorter)
  • The Three Forms of Unity
  • The Heidelberg Catechism
  • Keach's Catechism (1677)

The Puritans

  • John Bunyan — The Pilgrim's Progress; Grace Abounding
  • John Flavel — Keeping the Heart
  • John Owen — The Mortification of Sin
  • Richard Sibbes — The Bruised Reed
  • Thomas Watson — All Things for Good

The Evangelical & Pastoral Tradition

  • Jonathan Edwards — Religious Affections
  • Andrew Murray — With Christ in the School of Prayer; Absolute Surrender; The True Vine
  • J. C. Ryle — Holiness; Expository Thoughts on the Gospels (Matthew)
  • Charles Spurgeon — Morning and Evening; The Treasury of David; Faith's Checkbook; All of Grace; Till He Come

Modern Pastoral

  • Selected devotional writings of Paul David Tripp

When you bring a question, your counselor draws from this library — the historic voice of the church speaking to your situation — and weaves it together with Scripture to point you to Christ, the Great Physician.

The Kiln Ministries

Formed in the fire.
For the glory of God.

The Kiln Ministries is a nonprofit ministry based in Nashville, Tennessee, committed to equipping believers with tools for biblical growth, counseling, and discipleship. Great Physician is one expression of that mission — bringing the healing Word of God to souls in need, wherever they are.

A Note of Honesty

Great Physician is not a replacement for your local church or pastor.

Barnabas, Lydia, Ethan, and Anna are tools to help you begin the journey toward biblical change. We will always encourage you to be part of a local congregation and to sit under faithful pastoral care. AI will never replace a physical local church and in-person counseling and discipleship.
